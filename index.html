The maker

One hide at a time.

I'm Varshita Namburi, a leather designer based in India, currently working with Calonge. Every piece here passes through my own hands, with no outsourced stitching and no laser-cut shortcuts.

I choose vegetable-tanned hides for the way they darken and soften with use, and I build for the person who'd rather repair a strap than replace a bag.

  • Full-grain & vegetable-tanned hides only
  • Saddle-stitched, never machine-sewn
  • Every piece signed and dated inside

How a piece is made

Four steps, no shortcuts

  1. 01

    Select the hide

    I run a hand over every skin for grain, thickness, and scar before it's approved for a piece.

  2. 02

    Pattern & cut

    Paper patterns are laid against the hide's natural give, cut with a head knife rather than a die press.

  3. 03

    Saddle-stitch

    Two needles, one thread, pulled through the same hole from both sides: a seam that won't unravel even if a thread breaks.

  4. 04

    Edge finish

    Edges are beveled, burnished with beeswax, and burnished again, leaving them smooth and sealed with no raw fibers left showing.
